About Me

LCpl. Zachary Spillers

Early Life

I was born in 1989 and raised in Tyler/East Texas. I grew up playing football and baseball, while always having my eyes on the military. My grandfather served in the Navy during WWII and to this day is still my hero and my reason for joining the military.

In high school, I joined the United States Naval Sea Cadet Corps were I got my first of the military. I did training with active duty Navy in Roswell AFB and Kingville NAS. During Hurricanes Katrina and Rita, I took part in "Operation Rita" and received a Certificate of Commendation from the Texas State Guard's Texas Medical Rangers, for providing medical assistance and security aide for evacuees during the huricanes.

The summer before my senior year of High School, I became a volunteer firefighter and on my 17th birthday I enlisted in the Marine Corps. I graduated from Whitehouse High School in 2007 and left for boot camp on October 15, 2007.

Marine Corps

I graduated from MCRD San Diego in January of 2008 and afer completing MCT, I reported to my MOS school at MCCES on MCB 29 Palms, CA. I spent nearly a year and half at communications and electronics school there before graduating as an Aviation Communication Systems Repair Technician and then reporting to MTACS-38 at MCAS Miramar, CA.

Although I was trained as a maintainer, I spent most of my time as a radio operator on numurous training missions before finally getting orders for Afghanistan. Unfortunately, my long awaited deployment and career in the Marine Corps was cut short due to a training mishap that nearly broke my back and almost paralyzed. Just shy of 2 and half years in and I was discharged and I am now 90% disabled by the VA.

Education

After the Marine Corps, I enrolled in Vocational Rehabillitation and enrolled in college. I obtained my Associates of Arts in History and attended the University of Texas at Tyler to pursue my Bachelors of Art in History and a minor in International Relations. Coding first caught my interest in high school when I took computer science and learned rudimentary JavaScript, but the military was my part so I did't pursue it any further at that time.

The past couple years I have been trying to get back into college with an attempt to get back into coding, but have met with several roadblocks, until a couple months ago I came across VetsWhoCode and decided to apply in an to reignite and old interest and possible future career path.
